Chapter 2: Randomized Experiments — Summary & Key Technical Points

2.1 Randomization

Introduction to Causal Effects and Randomization

  • Causal Question Example: Does you looking up make other people look up?
  • Setup: Randomly assign action via coin flip, compare outcomes (proportion of "look up" responses).
  • Result: 55% vs. 1% — strong evidence for a causal effect.

Key Concepts

  • Experiment: Researcher controls the action.
  • Randomization: Assignment determined by a random mechanism, not a deterministic rule.
  • Why Randomization?
    • Avoids confounding: ensures groups are comparable.
    • Critics cannot argue that group differences are driving the observed effect (unlike sex-based assignment).

The Problem of Missing Counterfactuals

  • In any real study, for an individual, only one potential outcome is observed:
    • Ya=1Y^{a=1}Ya=1 (under treatment)
    • Ya=0Y^{a=0}Ya=0 (under no treatment)
  • Only YYY under AAA (the treatment actually received) is observed. The other counterfactual is missing.

Table 2.1

  • Illustrates observed data and missing counterfactuals for each individual.

Randomization Generates Exchangeability

  • Exchangeability: Treated and untreated groups are comparable because any differences are due to chance.
  • Even if the treatment allocation is flipped after randomization, the estimation of effects remains unchanged.
  • Formalization: The distribution of outcomes under randomization makes:
    • Pr⁡[Ya=1=1∣A=1]=Pr⁡[Ya=1=1∣A=0]\Pr[Y^{a=1} = 1 | A=1] = \Pr[Y^{a=1} = 1 | A=0]Pr[Ya=1=1∣A=1]=Pr[Ya=1=1∣A=0]
    • Pr⁡[Ya=1=1∣A=1]=Pr⁡[Ya=1=1]\Pr[Y^{a=1} = 1 | A=1] = \Pr[Y^{a=1} = 1]Pr[Ya=1=1∣A=1]=Pr[Ya=1=1] (marginal risk)
    • More generally, for all aaa, Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp AYa⊥⊥A
  • Exogeneity is another term for exchangeability in this context.

Technical Point 2.1: Versions of Exchangeability

  • Full Exchangeability: Randomization produces joint independence between all potential outcomes and AAA.
    • YA⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^A \perp\!\!\!\perp AYA⊥⊥A (joint vector of all counterfactuals)
  • Mean Exchangeability: E[Ya∣A=1]=E[Ya∣A=0]E[Y^a|A=1] = E[Y^a|A=0]E[Ya∣A=1]=E[Ya∣A=0] for all aaa.
    • Sufficient for causal identification: E[Ya]=E[Y∣A=a]E[Y^a] = E[Y|A=a]E[Ya]=E[Y∣A=a]
  • For dichotomous outcomes, these are equivalent in practice, so simply called "exchangeability".

Important Distinction

  • Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp AYa⊥⊥A IS NOT Y⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY \perp\!\!\!\perp AY⊥⊥A:
    • If treatment has causal effect, then YYY and AAA will be associated, but the assignment of AAA is unrelated to potential outcomes.

Fine Point 2.1: Crossover Experiments

  • When each individual serves as their own control (receives both treatments at different times).
  • Individual causal effects can only be identified if:
    1. No carryover effects: Yi1a0,a1=Yi1a1Y^{a_0, a_1}_{i1} = Y^{a_1}_{i1}Yi1a0​,a1​​=Yi1a1​​
    2. Effects don't depend on time.
    3. Counterfactual under no treatment doesn't depend on time.
  • Not generally possible for irreversible treatments/outcomes (e.g., heart transplants).

2.2 Conditional Randomization

Conditional (Stratified) Randomization Designs

  • Design 1 (Marginal Randomization):
    • All individuals randomized with the same probability.
    • Ensures marginal exchangeability: Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp AYa⊥⊥A for all aaa.
  • Design 2 (Conditional Randomization):
    • Probability of treatment assignment differs by a baseline factor LLL (e.g., critical vs. noncritical condition).
    • Ensures conditional exchangeability: Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥A∣LY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp A \mid LYa⊥⊥A∣L, but not marginal exchangeability.

Definition

  • Conditional Exchangeability: For all lll,

    Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥A∣L=lY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp A \mid L=l Ya⊥⊥A∣L=l

    Or, in risk terms:

    Pr⁡[Ya=1∣A=1,L=l]=Pr⁡[Ya=1∣A=0,L=l],∀a,l\Pr[Y^a=1|A=1,L=l] = \Pr[Y^a=1|A=0,L=l], \forall a, l Pr[Ya=1∣A=1,L=l]=Pr[Ya=1∣A=0,L=l],∀a,l

  • Marginal exchangeability: Holds only if randomization is not conditional on LLL.

Data missing patterns:

  • MCAR: Data are 'Missing Completely At Random', e.g., in marginal randomization.
  • MAR: Data are 'Missing At Random' conditional on LLL, e.g., in conditional randomization.

Effect Estimation in Conditional Randomization

  • Two main options:
    1. Stratum-specific (stratified) average causal effects (effect modification)
      • Estimate average causal effect within each strata L=lL=lL=l.
    2. Marginal (overall) average causal effect
      • Average over the population distribution of LLL.

2.3 Standardization

Standardization Formula for the Marginal Causal Effect

  • Let LLL be a pre-treatment variable (e.g., critical status).
  • For each level lll, compute observed risks:

    Pr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]\Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=a] Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]

  • Marginal potential outcome risk:

    Pr⁡[Ya=1]=∑lPr⁡[Ya=1∣L=l]Pr⁡[L=l]\Pr[Y^a=1] = \sum_l \Pr[Y^a=1|L=l] \Pr[L=l] Pr[Ya=1]=l∑​Pr[Ya=1∣L=l]Pr[L=l]

  • Under conditional exchangeability:

    Pr⁡[Ya=1]=∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr⁡[L=l]\Pr[Y^a=1] = \sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=a] \Pr[L=l] Pr[Ya=1]=l∑​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr[L=l]

  • Causal risk ratio by standardization:

    Causal RR=∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr⁡[L=l]∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr⁡[L=l]\text{Causal RR} = \frac{\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=1] \Pr[L=l]} {\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=0] \Pr[L=l]} Causal RR=∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr[L=l]∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr[L=l]​

Standardized mean (in general):

Standardized mean:∑lE[Y∣L=l,A=a]Pr⁡[L=l]\text{Standardized mean:} \qquad \sum_l E[Y|L=l, A=a]\Pr[L=l] Standardized mean:l∑​E[Y∣L=l,A=a]Pr[L=l]

Fine Point 2.2: Risk Periods

  • Specification of the time-window for risk is critical (e.g. 5-day vs 100-year mortality risk yields different causal effect interpretations).

2.4 Inverse Probability Weighting (IPW)

Idea:

  • Create a pseudo-population where treatment is independent of LLL by weighting individuals inversely by their probability of receiving the treatment they actually received.

For each individual:

WA=1Pr⁡(A∣L)W_A = \frac{1}{\Pr(A|L)} WA​=Pr(A∣L)1​

  • For treated with L=lL=lL=l: Weight 1/Pr⁡(A=1∣L=l)1/\Pr(A=1|L=l)1/Pr(A=1∣L=l)
  • For untreated with L=l′:L=l':L=l′: Weight 1/Pr⁡(A=0∣L=l′)1/\Pr(A=0|L=l')1/Pr(A=0∣L=l′)

Marginal Outcome under Treatment aaa by IPW:

E∗[Y∣A=a]=E[Y⋅I(A=a)Pr⁡(A=a∣L)]E^\ast[Y|A=a] = E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{\Pr(A=a|L)}\right] E∗[Y∣A=a]=E[Pr(A=a∣L)Y⋅I(A=a)​]

Where I(A=a)\mathbb{I}(A=a)I(A=a) is indicator function for treatment.

Technical Point 2.2: Formal Definition

  • Using density notation, for discrete variables:

    f[a∣l]=Pr⁡(A=a∣L=l)f[a|l] = \Pr(A=a|L=l) f[a∣l]=Pr(A=a∣L=l)

    So

    WA=1f[A∣L]W_A = \frac{1}{f[A|L]} WA​=f[A∣L]1​

  • Expected (weighted) mean equivalence:

    E∗[Y∣A=a]=E[Y⋅I(A=a)f[A∣L]]E^\ast[Y|A=a] = E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{f[A|L]}\right] E∗[Y∣A=a]=E[f[A∣L]Y⋅I(A=a)​]

Equivalence of IP Weighting and Standardization (Technical Point 2.3):

  • Standardization:

    ∑lE[Y∣A=a,L=l]Pr⁡[L=l]\sum_l E[Y|A=a,L=l]\Pr[L=l] l∑​E[Y∣A=a,L=l]Pr[L=l]

  • IPW:

    E[Y⋅I(A=a)f[A∣L]]E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{f[A|L]}\right] E[f[A∣L]Y⋅I(A=a)​]

  • They are mathematically equivalent under positivity and conditional exchangeability.
  • Proof via properties of the expectation and indicator function.

Positivity Requirement

  • For all combinations of AAA and LLL with nonzero Pr⁡[L=l]\Pr[L=l]Pr[L=l], Pr⁡(A=a∣L=l)>0\Pr(A=a|L=l)>0Pr(A=a∣L=l)>0.

中文总结

2.1 随机化

  • 因果问题基础: 举例实际因果推断问题及如何设计随机化实验。
  • 随机化: 通过随机机制（如抛硬币）分配处理，消除了混杂。
  • 交换性（Exchangeability）: 随机化保证组间（处理/对照）可比性，即群体间唯一差异是随机。
    • 形式化：Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp AYa⊥⊥A 对所有 aaa 都成立。
  • 完全交换性/均值交换性: 随机化使所有潜在结局与处理独立，均值交换性即可用于均值效应识别。
  • 注意: Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp AYa⊥⊥A 不等价于 Y⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥AY \perp\!\!\!\perp AY⊥⊥A。如果确有因果效应，对 YYY 与 AAA 必然有关联。

精要点

  • 交叉实验不能通用: 只有在无携带效应、效应无时间依赖、对照结果稳定等极强条件下，个体因果效应才能被识别。

2.2 条件随机化

  • 边际随机化: 每人接受处理概率相同，保证整体交换性。
  • 条件随机化: 分层分配概率（如按疾病严重程度），仅保证层内交换性。
    • 条件交换性: Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥A∣LY^a \perp\!\!\!\perp A|LYa⊥⊥A∣L
  • 效应估计:
    • 层内（分层）平均因果效应
    • 总体（边际）平均因果效应（通过标准化或加权获得）

2.3 标准化

  • 总体潜在结局风险: 混合各层次风险的加权平均

    Pr⁡[Ya=1]=∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr⁡[L=l]\Pr[Y^a=1] = \sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=a] \Pr[L=l] Pr[Ya=1]=l∑​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr[L=l]

  • 因果风险比:

    ∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr⁡[L=l]∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr⁡[L=l]\frac{\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=1]\Pr[L=l]} {\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=0]\Pr[L=l]} ∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr[L=l]∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr[L=l]​

  • 标准化本质： 按总体 LLL 分布标准化不同处理组的结局风险。

2.4 逆概率加权（IPW）

  • 方法: 给个体赋予 1/Pr⁡(A∣L)1/\Pr(A|L)1/Pr(A∣L) 的权重来创造一个“处理与 LLL 独立”的伪总体。
    • 加权均值公式：

    E[Y⋅I(A=a)Pr⁡(A=a∣L)]E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{\Pr(A=a|L)}\right] E[Pr(A=a∣L)Y⋅I(A=a)​]

  • 权重定义: WA=1Pr⁡(A∣L)W_A = \frac{1}{\Pr(A|L)}WA​=Pr(A∣L)1​，依个体实际处理组和层次确定。
  • 与标准化等价: 数学上完全等价（需满足正则性，即各分组概率 >0>0>0）。
  • 与分层标准化理解: 两种方式本质等价，都是在消除（或校正）分层变量 LLL 影响之后求处理效果。

章节要点总结

  • 随机化消除混杂，赋予结果因果意义。
  • 交换性是识别平均因果效应的关键（均值交换性即可）。
  • 条件随机化下只保证分层内交换性，需用标准化或IP加权获得总体效应。
  • 标准化与逆概率加权本质等价，是现代因果推断分析核心。
  • 做因果推断时，明确处理、结局、分层变量及其分布关系至关重要。
  • 理想随机化实验很稀少，许多实际研究（如器官移植）因伦理、可行性等限制无法实施。

核心公式概览（中英文对照）

1. 平均处理效应/边际潜在结局：

Pr⁡[Ya=1]=∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr⁡[L=l]\Pr[Y^a=1] = \sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=a] \Pr[L=l] Pr[Ya=1]=l∑​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=a]Pr[L=l]

  • (The marginal potential outcome risk is the weighted sum of stratum-specific observed risks.)

2. 标准化风险比：

Causal Risk Ratio=∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr⁡[L=l]∑lPr⁡[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr⁡[L=l]\text{Causal Risk Ratio} = \frac{\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=1]\Pr[L=l]} {\sum_l \Pr[Y=1|L=l,A=0]\Pr[L=l]} Causal Risk Ratio=∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=0]Pr[L=l]∑l​Pr[Y=1∣L=l,A=1]Pr[L=l]​

3. 逆概率加权均值（IPW）：

E[Y⋅I(A=a)Pr⁡(A=a∣L)]E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{\Pr(A=a|L)}\right] E[Pr(A=a∣L)Y⋅I(A=a)​]

  • (The IP-weighted estimator of the mean outcome under treatment aaa.)

4. 交换性定义：

Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥A(marginal)Y^a \perp\!\!\!\perp A \quad \text{(marginal)} Ya⊥⊥A(marginal)

Ya⊥ ⁣ ⁣ ⁣⊥A∣L(conditional)Y^a \perp\!\!\!\perp A \mid L \quad \text{(conditional)} Ya⊥⊥A∣L(conditional)

  • (Exchangeability: potential outcomes are independent of treatment (possibly conditioning on strata).)

5. 权重定义：

WA=1Pr⁡(A∣L)W_A = \frac{1}{\Pr(A|L)} WA​=Pr(A∣L)1​

  • (IP weights: inverse of the conditional probability of receiving the observed treatment given LLL.)

6. Equivalence of Standardization and IPW:

∑lE[Y∣A=a,L=l]Pr[L=l]=E[Y⋅I(A=a)Pr⁡(A=a∣L)]\sum_l E[Y|A=a,L=l] Pr[L=l] = E\left[\frac{Y \cdot \mathbb{I}(A=a)}{\Pr(A=a|L)}\right] l∑​E[Y∣A=a,L=l]Pr[L=l]=E[Pr(A=a∣L)Y⋅I(A=a)​]

  • (Under positivity and conditional exchangeability.)
